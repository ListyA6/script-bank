# Bookkeeping: 12 hours a month to 30 minutes

> My restaurant's books took 12–16 hours a month because every number lived in a different place — attendance in a book, bonuses on a page, expenses and receipts in a pile, sales in the POS. I built one app everything feeds into, so closing the month is now one dashboard, one button, about 30 minutes.

## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

My restaurant's bookkeeping used to take twelve hours a month. Some months sixteen. Now it's about thirty minutes. Same restaurant, same numbers. And I wasn't slow at it. The problem was where everything lived. Attendance was in one book. Bonuses, I wrote those on a different page. Expenses and receipts were a pile on my desk. And the sales sat in the POS, separate from all of it. So closing the month meant collecting the same numbers from five places, by hand, every time. The math was never the hard part. The gathering was. So I built one app, and I made every part of the restaurant feed into it. The staff clock in there. Bonuses, expenses, a photo of a receipt, the sales. It all lands in the same place now. So the month isn't a job anymore. It's a screen. One dashboard, one button, and the whole month is just there. Labor, what we spent, what came in. Thirty minutes, and most of that is me just checking it looks right. Last month I closed the books waiting for my coffee. Didn't even open my laptop.
