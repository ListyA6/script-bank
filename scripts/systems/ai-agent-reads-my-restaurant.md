# AI agent reads my restaurant's data (ep. 2)

> Episode 2 of the data-app story. Ep 1 pooled all my restaurant's numbers into one app. This time I put an AI on top of that data and let it read. In two months it caught a dish that quietly stopped making money, shifts I'm overstaffed on, and which days actually spike — things I run the place and never noticed.

## Hooks — write 5+, pick 1
1. I connected an AI to my restaurant's data two months ago. It keeps catching things I never would've.
2. I gave an AI two months of my restaurant's numbers. It found money I was losing and never saw.
3. A dish I sell every day was barely making money. I didn't catch it. The AI did.
4. I built the app. Then I let an AI read it. Now it tells me what's wrong before I notice.
5. I don't check my dashboard anymore. I just ask the AI what's bleeding.

**Chosen hook:** #1 (leaning) — #3 is the more specific alt if testing

## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

I connected an AI to my restaurant's data two months ago. And it keeps telling me things I run the place and never noticed. Last video, I built one app that pulls all my numbers into one place. Sales, labor, expenses, all of it. So this time I just gave the whole thing to the AI and let it read. First week, it flagged a dish. One we sell every single day. The ingredient price had been creeping up for months, and that plate was barely making money anymore. I never saw it, because the total still looked fine. It saw it, because it reads every line. And that dish wasn't the only thing. It told me which shifts have too many people on. The slow afternoons where I'm paying more staff than the sales need. It found which days actually spike, and why. Turns out it's payday week, every time. Stuff I could've worked out myself, if I ever had the hours to sit and look. I don't. It does. So now I don't dig through the dashboard. I just ask it how last week went, or what's getting more expensive, or where I'm losing money. And it answers, because it's been reading the whole time. It caught that dish on a Tuesday. I changed the recipe Wednesday.
