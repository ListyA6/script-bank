# Hook Patterns for Operator/Builder Short-Form (First 3 Seconds)

Catalog of opening patterns observed in shorts from operator/builder creators (Pieter Levels, Marc Lou, Theo Browne, Greg Isenberg, Daniel Vassallo, Sam Parr, Tibo, Ben Lang) plus the short-form coaches whose frameworks they implicitly use (Alex Hormozi, Brendan Kane, Jon Davids). Audience here = builder-operator brand: ships real things, shows numbers, screenshares allowed.

---

### 1. Hard Number / Receipts-First
**Example:** Marc Lou — "I made $47,000 last month selling a Next.js boilerplate" (TikTok/X, see [Mikel Vu teardown](https://mikelvu.medium.com/marc-lou-the-indie-entrepreneurs-success-story-169f0de637c4)). Pieter Levels routinely opens tweets/clips with "$84k MRR with 0 employees."
**Why it works:** Specificity = credibility. Odd, non-round numbers ($38K in 10 days — [HN](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43252999)) read as real, not marketing. Activates social proof + greed instantly.
**Fits operator/builder when:** Numbers are real and you'll show a Stripe/dashboard screenshot in seconds 2–4 (Marc Lou literally built TrustMRR around this — [startupseries.io](https://startupseries.io/how-indie-hacker-marc-lou-monetised-mrr-bragging/)).
**Off-brand when:** You're a coach selling courses with no SKU behind the number. Reads as gurucore.

### 2. "I Built X in Y" Reveal
**Example:** Marc Lou — "I built and shipped TrustMRR in 24 hours." Tibo (@tibo_maker) — "I built a SaaS in a weekend, here's the stack." Theo — "I rebuilt our entire chat app in 3 days."
**Why it works:** Compresses effort visually before any explanation. Promises a build montage payoff. Triggers "wait, how?"
**Fits when:** You actually have shippable artifact to cut to. Pair with cursor/terminal screen in second 2.
**Off-brand when:** The "build" is a Notion doc. Show working software or don't use it.

### 3. Contrarian Claim
**Example:** Pieter Levels — "VCs are a scam for 99% of founders." Daniel Vassallo — "Quitting Amazon to make less money was the best decision I made." Hormozi formula: "If you're [doing common thing], you're falling behind." ([itsmostly.com](https://itsmostly.com/blog/alex-hormozis-content-strategy-hook-retain-and-reward-explained)).
**Why it works:** Brendan Kane's "contrarian hook" — viewer must confirm or refute, both commit them to watch ([Kane on X](https://x.com/BrendanKane2/status/1977136032073027925)).
**Fits when:** You've lived the contrarian path. Vassallo's small-bets stance only works because he left $500k/yr at Amazon ([Pathless Path](https://pathlesspath.com/vassallo-small-bets/)).
**Off-brand when:** Hot take with no scar tissue. Reads as engagement bait.

### 4. Curiosity Gap / Open Loop
**Example:** Greg Isenberg — "I found a $10K/mo business idea hiding in a subreddit nobody reads." Hormozi — "One of the reasons all my workouts are the same is…" Theo — "There's one Next.js setting that 10x'd our build time."
**Why it works:** Specific promise + withheld payoff. Kane: hook must be *specific* — vague = scroll ([prepublish.ai](https://prepublish.ai/blog/what-is-a-youtube-hook)).
**Fits when:** Payoff is a real tactic/number, not a soft "mindset shift."
**Off-brand when:** You bait then dump generic advice. Audience punishes second view.

### 5. Problem-Callout / Pain Mirror
**Example:** Sam Parr — "Most founders are one bad month from quitting and won't admit it." Tibo — "Your landing page is the reason nobody's paying you."
**Why it works:** Names a private fear out loud. Hormozi calls this "direct challenge to current actions" ([reap.video](https://www.reap.video/blog/3-simple-strategies-to-create-viral-youtube-shorts-inspired-by-alex-hormozi)).
**Fits when:** You can immediately show the fix from your own build.
**Off-brand when:** All diagnosis, no demo. Becomes LinkedIn thoughtleader-ese.

### 6. Fail Story / Public Loss
**Example:** Sam Parr's *"Sh*t, I'm F*cked"* event opener — "10 founders telling 5-min stories of utter misery. One raised $100M and blew it in 2 years." ([X](https://x.com/thesamparr/status/1991517686501707948)). Marc Lou — "I spent 2 years building a SaaS that COVID killed at $4K MRR." ([X](https://x.com/marc_louvion/status/1833849231301554378)).
**Why it works:** Inverts the success-porn pattern. Disarms cynicism, earns watch-through.
**Fits operator/builder perfectly:** Failure receipts (dead product URLs, killed Stripe accounts) are unfakeable.
**Off-brand when:** Performative failure with no number/date. Stays vague = read as humblebrag.

### 7. Behind-the-Scenes Tease
**Example:** Pieter Levels — "Here's my entire stack running Nomad List on one $40 VPS." Theo — "This is what our actual production dashboard looks like at 3am." Tibo screen-records his Cursor window mid-feature.
**Why it works:** Voyeurism. The promise = "see what nobody else shows."
**Fits when:** Visual itself is the hook — open on the actual screen, not your face.
**Off-brand when:** It's a curated "studio tour." Builders watch for mess, not aesthetics.

### 8. Listicle Promise
**Example:** Greg Isenberg — "5 startup ideas you can steal this weekend." Hormozi — "3 things I'd do if I lost everything tomorrow." Kane: numeric callouts create commitment ([instacarousel.com](https://instacarousel.com/blog/carousel-hooks-that-stop-the-scroll/)).
**Why it works:** Bounded scope = low cognitive cost to watch.
**Fits when:** List items are tactical and you cut fast (≤6s per item).
**Off-brand when:** Operator brand using clickbait numbers ("17 tips!"). Three to five only.

### 9. Controversial Opinion (Industry-Specific)
**Example:** Theo — "Tailwind is the best thing to happen to CSS, fight me." Pieter Levels — "AI will kill SaaS as we know it." 
**Why it works:** Tribal — instantly sorts viewers into camps, comments explode = algo signal.
**Fits when:** You're tech/category-credentialed enough to defend it through the video.
**Off-brand when:** Used to start a fight you can't finish. Damages trust faster than it builds reach.

### 10. Before/After Reveal
**Example:** Marc Lou — split-screen "$0 → $2K MRR in 7 days." Tibo — "Day 1 of my landing page vs Day 30." Build-in-public day-counters ([founderos.com](https://www.founderos.com/blog/how-i-write-viral-hooks)).
**Why it works:** Visual delta does the work — caption can be silent.
**Fits operator/builder strongly:** Dashboards, designs, code diffs all native to this format.
**Off-brand when:** Faked or aspirational. The whole pattern dies if one entry is staged.

## Spoken vs Caption-Only

- **Spoken hook + face**: best for opinion/contrarian/fail-story (1, 3, 5, 6, 9). Voice carries conviction. Hormozi-style ([sendshort.ai](https://sendshort.ai/guides/create-video-hormozi-style/)) — bold sentence + cut to b-roll by second 2.
- **Caption-only over screen**: best for build reveals, dashboards, before/after (2, 7, 10). Big top-third text, no narration first 2s — viewer reads, then audio kicks in. Works on muted autoplay (default on IG/TikTok).
- **Hybrid (caption + voice mismatch)**: caption states the claim, voice delivers a different teaser line. Greg Isenberg and Tibo both use this — doubles information density ([Isenberg on X](https://x.com/gregisenberg/status/1977828256750403927)).

## Face in Frame 1 vs Cut-to-Screen

**Show face first when:** contrarian opinion, fail story, pain-mirror — credibility is *you*. Tight crop, eyes locked, no intro animation.
**Cut straight to screen when:** number reveal (Stripe dash), build demo (Cursor/terminal), before/after diff. The artifact IS the proof. Pieter Levels often opens with a Nomad List analytics screen — no face, no music. Theo's most-watched shorts open mid-code-diff.
**Rule of thumb for operator/builder:** if the hook is a *claim*, show face. If the hook is *proof*, show the screen. Never open on a logo, intro card, or "hey guys."

## Volume Rule (Isenberg/Hormozi consensus)
Ship ≥10 hook variants per idea; let the algo pick the winner ([Isenberg](https://x.com/gregisenberg/status/1974555652740854110)). Pattern-interrupt every 2 seconds after the hook lands.
