# B-Roll Vocabulary — Operator/Builder Short-Form

Reference for a solo operator (restaurants + agency + bakery + code) shooting vertical on a phone. Every B-roll type below earns its place by proving something the voiceover claims. If it doesn't prove or punctuate, cut it.

## Core Principle
Operator/builder content lives on **proof density**. Levels, Marc Lou, Tibo win because the screen behind their face is doing work — a Stripe chart, a deploy log, a kitchen line. Greg Isenberg's "dopamine engineer" rule applies: something on screen changes every 1.5–3 seconds. For Listy, the unfair advantage is having *both* dashboards AND a physical ops floor — most builders only have one.

## The Vocabulary

### 1. Screenshare / App Demo / Code Scroll
- **Says:** "I actually built this."
- **Right when:** Talking about a feature, a bug, a launch, an AI prompt. Wrong when discussing strategy/mindset — feels like filler.
- **Length:** 1.5–3s per cut. Never let a static screen sit past 3s without zoom/pan/highlight.
- **Solo film:** Screen-record with built-in iOS recorder or Tella. Add a soft zoom-in on the part you're naming. Cursor circle on.

### 2. Dashboard & Metrics (Stripe, Analytics, Followers)
- **Says:** Authority. Skin in the game.
- **Right when:** Making a claim about revenue, growth, traffic, or a result. Wrong when the number is small and ungrounded — looks like flexing without context.
- **Length:** 1–2s. Long enough to read the number, not long enough to audit it.
- **Solo film:** Screenshot → drop into editor → Ken Burns zoom toward the key number. Blur side data if needed. Real > mocked, always.

### 3. Product-in-Hand (Physical)
- **Says:** "This is real, not a deck."
- **Right when:** Talking LalaFun geprek, Floural bread, Kosolo noodles, packaging. Wrong when topic is digital/strategic — physical cutaway feels random.
- **Length:** 1–2s, often as an opening hook or punchline cap.
- **Solo film:** Phone on tripod, top-down or 45° angle, natural window light. Move the product (place it, flip it, tear it) — movement beats static.

### 4. Workspace Establishing Shot
- **Says:** "Here's the world I operate from."
- **Right when:** Opening a video, transitioning scenes, intro to a new project. Wrong as mid-claim B-roll — establishing shots don't prove things.
- **Length:** 1–1.5s, usually first 3 seconds of the video.
- **Solo film:** Phone on tripod or shelf, ultrawide lens, lock the frame, walk into shot or pan slowly by hand. Shoot 3–4 of these once a month and reuse.

### 5. Hand-on-Laptop / Typing Close-up
- **Says:** Work is happening.
- **Right when:** Bridging between two talking-head clips, covering an edit cut, narrating "I sat down and built…". Wrong if hands are obviously fake-typing — viewers spot it.
- **Length:** 0.8–1.5s.
- **Solo film:** Phone on a side tripod, lens close to the keyboard, type something real (an actual message or commit). Two angles: over-shoulder and side profile.

### 6. Behind-the-Scenes (Kitchen, Staff, Ops Floor)
- **Says:** "I run things." This is Listy's moat. Few builders have this.
- **Right when:** Talking ops, hiring, scale, margins, restaurant business. Wrong when audience is pure tech — they don't decode it.
- **Length:** 1.5–2.5s. Movement-heavy footage can hold slightly longer.
- **Solo film:** Phone handheld, walk through the line during prep or service. Get hands cooking, tickets printing, staff moving. Always ask permission, never film customers' faces.

### 7. Phone-in-Hand UI Shots
- **Says:** "This is the customer experience."
- **Right when:** Showing GoFood orders, WhatsApp messages, a live notification, Floural IG. Wrong when content is desktop-native.
- **Length:** 1–2s.
- **Solo film:** Second phone records the primary phone. Use a small tripod with a horizontal arm. Tilt screen 10° to kill glare.

### 8. Whiteboard / Paper Sketch
- **Says:** "I think in systems."
- **Right when:** Explaining a model, a funnel, unit economics. Wrong when overused — becomes the lazy-thinker visual.
- **Length:** 2–3s with a hand drawing actively (motion sells it).
- **Solo film:** Top-down phone mount over a notebook. Draw in real time, no pre-drawn fakes — the viewer can tell.

### 9. Stock / Archive Footage
- **Says:** Filler. Use sparingly.
- **Right when:** Referencing a city, era, news event, or concept (AI, crypto, supply chain) you can't shoot. Wrong as default — stock screams "I have nothing real."
- **Length:** Under 1s if possible. In and out.
- **Solo film:** Pexels, Pixabay, or your own archive folder. Operators should have their own ever-growing B-roll bank that beats stock 9/10.

### 10. Memes / Reaction Inserts
- **Says:** "I'm online, I get it." Punchline punctuation.
- **Right when:** After a hot take or surprise stat. Wrong on serious topics (revenue, hiring decisions, losses) — kills credibility.
- **Length:** 0.5–1s. Slam in, slam out.
- **Solo film:** Save a folder of 30–50 reaction GIFs/clips. Drop in during edit.

### 11. Infographic / Text-on-Screen
- **Says:** Reinforces the spoken number.
- **Right when:** Any claim with a number, list, or comparison. Wrong when it duplicates the caption — pick one.
- **Length:** Match the spoken phrase, usually 1.5–2.5s.
- **Solo film:** Editor overlay (CapCut, Premiere). Big, one-line, bold sans. Animate in with a snap, not a fade.

### 12. Old Photos / Throwbacks
- **Says:** "I earned the right to talk about this." Origin-story currency.
- **Right when:** Year-in-review, "started from", lessons-learned, first-outlet stories. Wrong as recurring B-roll — keep it rare so it lands.
- **Length:** 1.5–2s, often with a slow zoom.
- **Solo film:** Pull from camera roll. Add light grain or a date stamp overlay so it reads as "old".

## The Proof Shot Pattern
This is the operator's signature move. Mid-video, the talking head freezes or shrinks to corner while a real screenshot — Stripe MRR, POS day-total, GoFood orders, follower spike — slams full-screen with a green circle on the key number. Hold 1.5–2s, then back to face.

Rules so it doesn't break flow:
1. **Land it on a claim, not a tangent.** "We did 130 juta last month" → proof shot. Not "I think marketing is hard" → proof shot.
2. **One key number, circled.** Eyes need a target in under a second.
3. **Keep audio continuous.** Don't pause the voiceover — the cutaway is visual only, the talking continues underneath.
4. **Don't over-clean.** Slight imperfection (other tabs visible, real timestamp) reads as authentic. Heavily mocked PNGs read as fake.
5. **Earn it once per video.** Two proof shots max in a 60-second piece. More and it looks like flexing.

## Listy's Unfair Stack
Most builders show only dashboards. Most restaurant owners show only kitchens. Listy can cut between a Stripe chart and a wok firing in the same 30 seconds — that contrast IS the brand. Shoot both worlds in every filming day.
