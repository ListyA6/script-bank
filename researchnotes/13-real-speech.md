# 13 — What Real Viral Reels Actually Sound Like

**Date:** 2026-06-09
**Source:** 224 transcribed Instagram/TikTok reels in `creator-research/videos/ig_round/transcripts/` (Vol. 2 dataset) — read directly, not summarized. This file corrects three things the kit got wrong, because Listy kept feeling scripts were "AI-y" even after the on-the-nose fix.

---

## Finding 1: Real reels don't HAVE a crafted closing line

They just stop when the point is delivered. Evidence, last lines verbatim:
- **Chris Donnelly** (3 realities about getting paid more): ends "...build a network of people you look up to and a network of people who are similar stage to you." → the third point, then stop. No wrap.
- **Codie Sanchez** (AI in business): ends "...if you guys aren't using this, you will get left behind. And that's the only thing I know for sure, even though I don't want it to be true." → finishes the thought with an honest reluctant tail, not an aphorism.
- **Chris Williamson** (infidelity stats): ends "...they have more opportunity to have an affair." → last fact, done.
- **Andy Frisella**: ends "...guess what the universe gonna give you? More hard shit." → stops on the last beat of the rant.

**The rule:** there is no payoff beat. Kill it. End on the last real thing that happens in the story, said plainly, then the video ends. Even a quiet "natural" button ("so I started on a Thursday") is still *writing a close* — and it reads as AI/unclear. If the last line feels a little unfinished, that's correct.

---

## Finding 2: They speak in long, chained, messy sentences — not fragments

The kit's "4-9 word sentence units" got over-applied into staccato chop ("One offer. Zero clients. That's it."), which is its OWN AI tell. Real example, one breath:
> "To the point that I hired a person 100% full time in my office who all he does is think about how do we integrate AI into my businesses." — Codie Sanchez

Real speech chains clauses with connective tissue: *and, so, because, to the point that, which is why, and so.* It runs long, then snaps short, then runs long again.

**The rule:** the 4-9 word target is about individual *clauses*. Chain them into real sentences. Vary length hard. A script where every line is a clipped fragment is wrong.

---

## Finding 3: Nobody recites their own revenue

When numbers appear, they're a **contrast about a cost/tool**, not a personal flex:
> "I don't hire investment analysts anymore... that was a $175,000 a year job. Gone. With a $200 a month Perplexity connection." — Codie Sanchez

She never says "I make $X." Listy hates saying rupiah out loud for the same reason — saying your own revenue is weird and flex-y.

**The rule:** money/rupiah figures go on the **screen** (the proof shot), never in the spoken track. Voice refers to scale in words — "my busiest shop," "the small one," "the kitchen that supplies the others." Spoken numbers are fine ONLY for counts and dates (plates, staff, "the 11th," "three days") — those don't feel weird.

## What this does NOT change

- Open on a scene (still true — real reels drop straight in).
- Stranger test (still true — no insider names).
- Document, don't advise (still true).
- The voice patterns in §1 (statement+complication, naming the loop, etc.) — still his fingerprint. This file is about cadence, endings, and numbers, not his personality.
