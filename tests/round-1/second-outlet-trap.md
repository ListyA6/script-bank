# The Second Outlet Trap

> Founders who think the second outlet will be easier than the first. It isn't. The systems and the people that carried the first one don't automatically copy over to the second.

## Hooks — write 5+, pick 1
1. Everyone says the second shop is easier. It's not. It's the same amount of hard, just in a new place.
2. *(chosen)* I thought the second restaurant would be easier because I already knew how to do it. I didn't know how to do it without the three people who made the first one work.
3. The first shop ran itself. I thought I built a system. I didn't. I built a team.
4. Opening a second restaurant felt like a copy-paste. Turns out people don't paste.
5. I had a working kitchen. I thought that meant I knew how to open one. Those are different things.
6. Six months into the second outlet I realised my system wasn't a system. It was one guy named Adi.

**Chosen hook:** I thought the second restaurant would be easier because I already knew how to do it. I didn't know how to do it without the three people who made the first one work.

## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

I thought the second restaurant would be easier because I already knew how to do it. I didn't know how to do it without the three people who made the first one work.

The first outlet has my kitchen lead. He's been with me two years, he knows the supplier, he knows when to pull a cook off the fryer without being told. The second one didn't have that guy.

So what I actually opened was a kitchen that needed me there every day.

I thought the SOP would cover it. I had it written down, steps, timings, all of it. But an SOP doesn't tell a new cook what to do when the supplier shows up two hours early and the walk-in is still full. That's not a document problem. And I wasn't there to fix it.

Three weeks in, my phone was louder from the second shop than from the first, and the first has four times the orders.

I ended up pulling my sous-chef from the first kitchen to stabilise the second one, which meant the first shop had to slow its prep. So opening the second one cost the first one two months of momentum.

I'm rebuilding the bench at the first shop now. Fourteen people spread across two kitchens and I'm basically hiring for a role I forgot I needed.
