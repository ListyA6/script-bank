## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

Day one of my first restaurant: I stood there for six hours and served maybe eight orders. This was LalaFun. Ayam geprek, small shop in Kediri, no delivery app yet, just a sign and a hope. I basically ran the counter myself. My girlfriend helped. We made the food, we packed it, we cleaned up. And at 9pm we had maybe eight orders on the receipt roll. I thought, okay, maybe I got the location wrong. Maybe the price. I didn't know which one. So I just kept opening. Every day for the first three months it was like that — slow mornings, quiet afternoons, a small spike at lunch. And I was doing everything: cooking, ops, talking to the one supplier we had. I had no system. I had a notebook and a phone. The shop that runs now, that I barely need to be inside — it's the same shop. Now we've got a kitchen team, a POS that logs every order, and I can check from wherever I am. Last Tuesday the shop served over 1,200 plates without me touching a single one. Day one it was eight orders. And we didn't close.
