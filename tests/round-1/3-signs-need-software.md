## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

Most businesses asking me to build them software don't need it yet.

I run a software agency, but I also run three restaurants, so I've lived both sides of this. And there's a test I use pretty fast.

Sign one: you're doing the same manual task more than once a day and it's eating someone's time. Not once a week. Every single day. My noodle shop had staff writing orders twice — once on paper, once in the POS. That's the sign.

But here's where people get it wrong.

Sign two: your workaround breaks when volume goes up. A spreadsheet that's fine for thirty orders starts cracking at three hundred, and you know it's cracking because you're patching it by hand. Sign three: two systems don't talk to each other, so someone copies data between them every single day.

But here's the one that gets skipped.

The sign you don't need software: you haven't written the process down yet. I've had clients come to me wanting a booking system, and I ask them to walk me through exactly how a booking gets confirmed right now. They can't. Not because it's complicated — because nobody wrote it down. A spreadsheet would've done it. We would've built a very expensive spreadsheet.

So I give them the spreadsheet first. If they outgrow it in three months, then we talk.

## Notes
- **Pronunciation flags:** "spreadsheet" — two clean syllables, no cluster issue. "Cracking" — clean. "Workaround" — stress on first syllable. "Complicated" — four syllables, fine for Listy but keep it as the one high-effort word in that sentence. Avoid stacking it near another long word.
- **Music / SFX:** No music or low neutral bed. Let the ambient kitchen noise from Kosolo B-roll breathe under Body A. Cut to silence or near-silence for the whiteboard shot — the contrast helps the "nobody wrote it down" land.
- **Shoot note:** The re-hook face returns should feel like genuine mid-thought interruptions, not a presenter segue. Quick cut, mid-sentence energy. No smile reset between B-roll and face return.
- **On the close:** Ends on a concrete condition and a fact ("three months," "then we talk") — not a lesson. The viewer assembles "build only when you've outgrown the simple thing" themselves. Do not add commentary.
