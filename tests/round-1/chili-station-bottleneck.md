## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

People think the fryer controls speed. It doesn't. This does.

This is the chili-grinding station at LalaFun, my ayam geprek shop. We start at 9. If this runs late, everything runs late.

Ayam geprek is the dish — you fry the chicken, smash it with sambal. The sambal has to be fresh, ground that morning. We're making about 300 portions before the lunch rush, so this station is going for two hours straight.

But here's where it actually breaks.

If one of the two grinders is slow, or we run out of raw chili mid-batch, the whole line waits. The fryer's ready, the rice is ready, the staff's standing there. We're just waiting on sambal.

So now I time this station every morning. If we're not done grinding by 11, I know service is going to be rough. That's basically the whole system.

## Notes
- **Pronunciation flags:** "ayam geprek" — AH-yam geh-PREK, keep it natural. "sambal" — SAM-bal. "cobek" — cho-BEK. "LalaFun" — LAH-lah-fun. "warung" — wah-ROONG. These are all Indonesian; don't over-anglicize them.
- **Music / SFX:** Low ambient kitchen sound underneath — the rhythmic grinding of the cobek, the scrape of the ulek. No music or a very low neutral loop. The grinding sound itself is the texture.
- **Shoot note:** Open on the B-roll, not face. The hook is said over the tight chili-station shot. This is bts hook_type — the thing is the opening frame, not the presenter.
