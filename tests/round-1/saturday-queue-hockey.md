# Saturday queue at 7pm

> A Saturday night at the busy shop. The queue forming at 7pm is the operator's version of a hockey-stick growth chart — visible, physical proof of demand.

4. I don't need a Stripe graph to know it's working. I just watch the queue form.
5. Investors ask for a hockey-stick chart. Mine forms in front of the shop at seven o'clock.

## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

You want to see a growth chart? Come to my shop at 7pm on a Saturday.

LalaFun, the ayam geprek place, the busy one. Saturday is our biggest night and the queue starts before the rush hour even hits. By 7:15 there's already eight, nine people waiting. They didn't come because of an ad. A lot of them are repeats, I see the same faces.

But here's the part that's actually hard to see until you're the one running the kitchen.

That queue means the kitchen is at capacity, and if my prep cook is two minutes slow, or the oil isn't hot enough, people bail. So the queue is proof of demand, but it's also exactly where everything breaks. We had one Saturday where the fryer went down at 7:30. Lost maybe forty orders in twenty minutes. And I wasn't even there that night. I was at the other shop.

So now I've got a Saturday checklist, fryers, oil temp, prep count, done at 6:30, before the queue shows up. And I get a photo sent to me at 6:45.

The queue still forms. I just don't get the calls anymore.
