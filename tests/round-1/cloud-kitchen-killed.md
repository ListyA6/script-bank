## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

Six weeks, then I killed the whole thing. The food was fine. The numbers weren't.

It was a delivery-only test — no dine-in, no tables. Just orders through the app, one kitchen, two staff.

I figured lower overhead meant better margin. No front-of-house, no servers, smaller space. And that part was true.

But I missed something on the packaging side.

Every order ships in a box, a seal sticker, a sauce cup, a bag. It adds up per order in a way it doesn't when someone sits down and eats off a plate. And because it's delivery, you can't cut it — the food has to arrive looking right or the ratings tank. So the packaging cost was basically fixed, and the margin per order was just too thin to survive.

I ran it for six weeks waiting for it to click. It didn't click.

So I added up the six weeks and closed it. The two staff moved over to one of my other kitchens.

Still have the leftover boxes. About 200 of them.
