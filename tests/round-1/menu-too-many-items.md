# Your menu is why your kitchen is slow

> A restaurant owner blames the kitchen for being slow, but the real bottleneck is the menu size. Cutting items — not adding cooks — is what actually speeds things up.

## Hooks — write 5+, pick 1
1. Your kitchen isn't slow. Your menu is too long.
2. I cut 12 items off the menu and the kitchen got faster the next day.
3. Hiring more cooks won't fix a menu problem.
4. The busiest hour at my shop used to take 18 minutes per table. Then I cut the menu.
5. Everyone blames the cooks. I blamed the menu. Turns out I was right.
6. Your kitchen speed problem is actually a menu problem.

**Chosen hook:** I cut 12 items off the menu and the kitchen got faster the next day.

## Voiceover — clean read
<!-- The full spoken track, exactly the way it's said. Read it aloud once. -->

I cut 12 items off the menu and the kitchen got faster the next day.

We were running 34 items at LalaFun. Every cook had to remember everything, so every order slowed everyone else down. I thought we needed one more cook. My manager said the same thing. But the real problem was the menu — too many combinations, too many ways to mess up.

So I pulled the 12 slowest ones. Not the unpopular ones. The ones that cost us three minutes every time someone ordered them.

Now we're at 22 items.
